National Museum of Anthropology

About National Museum of Fine Arts

The National Museum of Anthropology stages the Philippine ethnographic and terrestrial

and underwater archaeological collections narrating the story of the Philippines from the past,

presented through artifacts as evidences of its pre-history.


It is located in the Agrifina Circle, Rizal Park, Manila adjacent to the National Museum of Fine Arts building.

Built c. 1916-1918 from a neoclassical design by Canadian-American architect Ralph Harrington Doane when he was consulting architect to the

Philippine government, building formerly housed the Department of Finance. It also houses the wreck of the San Diego, ancient artifacts, and zoology divisions.
